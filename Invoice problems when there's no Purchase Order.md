# 1️⃣ Time Inefficiency (The Visible Cost)

Without a PO, admin must:

- Read invoice
    
- Identify model
    
- Search system
    
- Ask sales who ordered it
    
- Find deposit
    
- Create product
    
- Enter cost
    
- Add freight
    
- Add surcharges
    
- Assign department
    
- Localize
    
- Attach VIN
    
- Link to customer (if presold)
    

With a PO, most of that is already prepared.

Multiply that by:

- 20–40 bikes/month
    
- 12 months/year
    

That’s dozens of hours of skilled admin time spent reconstructing intent instead of processing.

But that’s the small part.

---

# 2️⃣ Cost Accuracy Leakage (The Invisible Margin Killer)

When inventory is created manually:

- Freight may be forgotten
    
- Environmental fees may be miscoded
    
- Campaign support may not be allocated
    
- Currency differences may not be handled consistently
    

Even small mistakes (200–500 SEK per unit) distort:

- Gross margin per bike
    
- Department profitability
    
- Salesperson performance reports
    

At volume, that’s real money.

---

# 3️⃣ Timing Distortion (Financial Reporting Risk)

With no PO:

- Inventory is created when invoice arrives
    
- Not when the order was committed
    
- Not when the unit shipped
    

This affects:

- Inventory aging
    
- Cash flow forecasting
    
- Floorplan interest tracking
    
- Open order reporting
    

Your financial reporting becomes reactive instead of predictive.

---

# 4️⃣ Accountability Blur

When no PO exists:

You cannot clearly see:

- Who ordered the unit
    
- When it was approved
    
- At what expected cost
    
- Whether it was stock or customer-specific
    

The system loses traceability.

This matters when:

- A wrong bike was ordered
    
- A discount was promised
    
- Margin is too low
    
- A unit sits unsold
    

Without PO history, you can’t trace the decision.

---

# 5️⃣ Control Weakness (The Risk Layer)

From an internal control perspective:

Creating inventory from an invoice means:

- The person entering cost controls the financial data
    
- There was no pre-approved cost expectation
    
- No separation between authorization and recording
    

In larger businesses, auditors don’t like this structure.

# What Happens With Proper PO Workflow

Clean flow:

1. Sales submits order
    
2. Admin creates PO in DLSoftware
    
3. PO shows as “Open”
    
4. Bike ships
    
5. Invoice arrives

7. System shows:

    - Due date
        
    - Payment status
        

Now you track payment inside the system — not on paper.

No printing needed for control purposes.
