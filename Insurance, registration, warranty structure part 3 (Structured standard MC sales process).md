# AVA MC – Standard Operating Procedure

## Customer Motorcycle Sales Process (Sweden)

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the standardized process for handling customer motorcycle purchases at AVA MC, ensuring:

- Legal compliance
    
- Financial control
    
- Proper registration and insurance handling
    
- Correct warranty activation
    
- Clear internal responsibility flow
    

This process applies to all motorcycle retail sales, including stock units and factory-ordered units.

---

# 2. Sales Process Overview

The motorcycle sales process consists of the following phases:

1. Customer Interest & Qualification
    
2. Offer & Agreement
    
3. Financing & Payment Control
    
4. Vehicle Preparation
    
5. Insurance & Registration
    
6. Warranty Activation
    
7. Delivery
    

Each phase must be completed in sequence unless otherwise specified.

---

# 3. Detailed Process Steps

---

## Phase 1 – Customer Interest & Qualification

### 1.1 Initial Contact

The customer may:

- Visit the dealership
    
- Respond to a Blocket advertisement
    
- Inquire via AVA MC website
    

### 1.2 Test Ride (If Applicable)

If the customer wishes to test ride:

- A test driving permit is completed and signed.
    
- The dealership does not register the customer in the sales system unless the test ride progresses toward a purchase discussion.
    

---

## Phase 2 – Offer & Purchase Agreement

### 2.1 Offer Creation

If the customer expresses purchase interest:

- An offer is created in the system.
    
- The offer includes:
    
    - Motorcycle model
        
    - VIN (if available)
        
    - Accessories / spare parts
        
    - Trade-in (if applicable)
        
    - Total price
        
    - Financing structure (if applicable)
        

### 2.2 Purchase Agreement

If the customer accepts the offer:

- A formal purchase agreement is created and signed.
    
- The agreement should specify:
    
    - Final price
        
    - Down payment amount
        
    - Financing terms (if applicable)
        
    - Estimated delivery timing
        

Note:  
If financing is required, the agreement is conditional upon financing approval.

---

## Phase 3 – Financing & Payment Control

### 3.1 Financing Application

If the customer chooses financing:

- A financing application is submitted to LF Finans.
    
- Credit check and approval decision are obtained.
    

No further irreversible steps may occur before financing approval.

### 3.2 Financing Approval

If financing is:

- Approved → proceed.
    
- Declined → renegotiate or cancel agreement.
    

### 3.3 Down Payment

Before proceeding to registration:

- Down payment must be received and confirmed.
    
- Or financing structure must be contractually finalized.
    

The deal is considered financially secure only after:

- Credit approval
    
- Down payment secured
    

---

## Phase 4 – Vehicle Sourcing & Preparation

There are three possible sourcing scenarios:

1. Motorcycle in stock → allocated to customer.
    
2. Pre-ordered unit → linked to customer.
    
3. Ordered from manufacturer → unit ordered through OEM system.
    

If ordered from manufacturer:

- VIN will be assigned once available.
    
- Delivery timeline is communicated to customer.
    

The motorcycle is prepared for delivery once:

- VIN is confirmed
    
- Financing is approved
    
- Down payment is secured
    

---

## Phase 5 – Insurance & Registration

### 5.1 Insurance

Insurance handling rules:

- Insurance may be quoted before agreement.
    
- Insurance may be prepared after agreement.
    
- Traffic insurance must be activated before registration.
    

Insurance is VIN-based.  
Insurance cannot be activated without a valid VIN.

Insurance activation must occur before registration.

---

### 5.2 Registration

Registration is completed via Bilvision and submitted to  
Swedish Transport Agency.

Registration may only occur after:

1. Financing approval (if applicable)
    
2. Down payment received
    
3. Active traffic insurance confirmed
    
4. VIN confirmed
    
5. Vehicle physically available
    

Registration legally transfers ownership to the customer.

No registration may occur before financial security is established.

---

## Phase 6 – Warranty Activation

Factory warranty activation occurs via the manufacturer’s portal.

Warranty activation must:

- Be completed after registration
    
- Be aligned with delivery date
    
- Not be activated prematurely (to avoid early warranty start)
    

Warranty start date typically corresponds to:

- First retail registration date
    

Extended warranties (if sold):

- Are registered at or immediately prior to delivery.
    

---

## Phase 7 – Final Delivery

Before handover, confirm:

- Financing fully approved
    
- Down payment secured
    
- Registration completed
    
- Insurance active
    
- Warranty registered
    
- Accessories installed
    
- PDI completed
    

Customer signs delivery confirmation.

Keys, documentation, and ownership documents are handed over.

Motorcycle is delivered.

---

# 4. Compliance & Control Principles

The following principles must always be followed:

- No delivery without active insurance.
    
- No registration without financing approval (if financed).
    
- No warranty activation before confirmed delivery.
    
- No vehicle release before payment security.
    

---

# 5. Risk Control Summary

Critical checkpoints in the process:

|Control Point|Risk Prevented|
|---|---|
|Financing approval before registration|Financial loss|
|Down payment secured before registration|Payment default risk|
|Insurance activated before registration|Legal non-compliance|
|Warranty activated at delivery|Loss of warranty coverage|
|VIN confirmed before insurance|Policy invalidation|

---

Administration:
