# 🔹 1️⃣ Is the “Purchase List” the Same as a Purchase Order?

Short answer:

👉 **Yes in function.**  
👉 **Not always in legal/technical form.**

What your dealership calls a **purchase list / requisition** in DLsoftware is essentially:

> An internal purchase order draft.

It serves as:

- A control document
    
- A checklist
    
- A financial pre-commitment record
    
- A reconciliation reference
    

But it only becomes a true **legal purchase order** once the order is placed in the OEM portal.

So in your workflow:

DL Purchase List → Portal Order → Supplier Invoice

The legally binding commitment usually happens when the portal order is submitted.

---

# 🔹 2️⃣ Why They Do It This Way (Spare Parts Flow)

Let’s simplify your spare parts process:

### Step 1 – Automatic Backorder Scan

DLsoftware checks:

- Workshop demand
    
- Customer orders
    
- Minimum stock levels
    

This generates suggested items.

---

### Step 2 – Purchase List Created (Internal Reference Number)

DL generates:  
`PL-2026-00458`

This list:

- Locks which parts should be ordered
    
- Creates traceability
    
- Prevents forgetting items
    
- Allows later comparison
    

This is internal control.

---

### Step 3 – Items Entered in OEM Portal

Staff manually re-enters items into:

- Yamaha portal
    
- BMW portal
    
- Honda portal  
    etc.
    

Portal generates:  
`OEM Order #873421`

Now the supplier sees the order.

This is the **real supplier commitment**.

---

### Step 4 – Delivery & Verification

When goods arrive:

- Staff checks items against portal order number
    
- Also checks against purchase list reference
    
- Confirms quantities
    
- Books supplier invoice
    

This prevents:

- Missing parts
    
- Double ordering
    
- Pricing mistakes
    

---

# 🔥 So What Is the Purchase List REALLY?

It’s an **internal control layer** between:

Inventory system (DLsoftware)  
and  
Supplier system (OEM portal)

Think of it as:

> “What we intend to buy”  
> before  
> “What we actually ordered”

---

# 🔹 3️⃣ Does It Work the Same With Motorcycles?

Now it gets interesting.

## Spare Parts

High volume  
Frequent ordering  
Small values  
Needs internal batching

So purchase lists are critical.

---

## Motorcycles

Low volume  
High value  
Often pre-sold  
Ordered individually

So the workflow is usually simpler.

---

# 🔹 Typical Motorcycle Flow

For motorcycles, dealerships often:

1. Log into OEM portal directly
    
2. Order the bike
    
3. Portal order number becomes the reference
    
4. When invoice arrives, match to portal order
    

They often **skip internal purchase lists** because:

- One bike = one order
    
- Easier to track manually
    
- Less frequent
    

---

# 🔹 When Do Dealers Use Internal POs for Motorcycles?

They create internal purchase orders when:

- They need strict accounting control
    
- The accounting department requires PO matching
    
- They want better cash flow forecasting
    
- They want approval workflow before ordering
    

In that case:

DL Purchase Order → Portal Order → Supplier Invoice

Just like spare parts — but less automated.

---

# 🔥 The Real Difference Between Parts and Motorcycles

|Spare Parts|Motorcycles|
|---|---|
|Batch ordering|Single-unit ordering|
|High frequency|Low frequency|
|Stock replenishment driven|Sales or allocation driven|
|Purchase list critical|Often optional|
|Many line items|One unit per order|

---

# 🧠 The Key Concept

Your dealership’s “purchase list” is:

An internal purchasing control document.

It becomes a real purchase commitment only when the order is submitted in the OEM portal.

For spare parts → It’s essential.  
For motorcycles → It’s often simplified or skipped.
