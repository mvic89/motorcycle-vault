##### Step by step process of customer motorcycle purchase:
- The customer comes in and wants to buy a bike, or he / she shows their interest of a bike through Blocket annons (which is connected to AVA MC's website).
- If the customer is interested in the bike, an 'offer' is created. The offer has all the customer information and additional information about the motorcycle / spare parts costs.
- If the customer accepts the offer, a 'purchase agreement' is created.
- Once the purchase agreement has been signed, AVA MC starts to make the motorcycle ready for delivery (if it's in stock), or order the motorcycle / the spare parts / equipment etc. if they don't have it in stock.
- There's 3 scenarios:
	- They can order the bike the customer wants directly from the motorcycle brand's ordering system. (if it's in stock of course)
	- They can link the customer to the pre-ordered bike if it's already pre-ordered.
	- If they have the motorcycle in stock, then it's sold. (which they usually try to convince the customer to go for.)
	 
- Insurance can be handled both before / after the agreement depending on the bike (if it's a sportbike with a high insurance fee, then the customer might want to have the figures set beforehand.)
- Registration is done after the purchase. (when down payment is paid and the vehicle is fully financed)
- When the motorcycle is ready to be delivered, the registration at 'Transportstyrelsen' through 'Bilvision' is made and warranties are registered through the motorcycle brand's portal. (different brands have different lengths on their warranties) (Kawasaki has 4-year warranties for instance.)
- Now the motorcycle is ready to be delivered or picked up by the customer.

##### Additional notes

When you trade in the motorcycle, the state of the vehicle is checked (tire wear, service documents etc.). With newer bikes, AVA takes a look at the receipts and double checks with the brand if service has been done. (every brand has their own service database).

When the customer wants AVA MC to sell their bike for them, the customer is registered on the bike until it's been sold. AVA MC helps out with finding potential buyers and advertising the bike on Blocket. (In extension their own website.)

Blocket and AVA MC's website are interconnected so when a motorcycle is in stock, they put up a 'Blocket Advert' with 'Blocket Admin' with all the bike information. After that, the advert data is sent and presented on the website.

If a customer is interested in one of AVA MC motorcycles on Blocket, they correspond through email with Tony or Gunnar. This notice of interest is printed out and kept in a cover.

As stated earlier, they put up the available motorcycles through 'Blocket Admin' on their website. this has to be done manually after the bikes has been registered into the database. It should be automatically done so that when they register a bike into the stock, it should automatically appear on the website (No manually created Blocket adverts needed!).

The customer usually pays the down payment through 'Swish' or 'Bankgiro' which is more beneficial for AVA MC due to lower banking fees. The possibility to use credit card still works though it's not encouraged.

Bankgiro is mostly used with later deliveries (not when the customer picks up the bike directly from the store). The account details are sent by email.

Tony wants to have the 'Bankgiro' information as well as 'Swish' and 'Account number' available on the purchase agreement.
