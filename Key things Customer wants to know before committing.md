The answer it gave me:

Great question — this is exactly where a lot of motorcycle dealerships either win or lose the sale.

When a customer sees an advert for a bike (new or used), the ad sparks **interest** — but it rarely removes all uncertainty. Before they’re ready to accept an offer, most buyers are trying to reduce _risk_ and justify the purchase emotionally and financially.

Here are the **key things customers typically want to know before committing**:

## 1. The Real Price (Not Just the Advertised Price)

This is the #1 friction point.

Customers want clarity on:

- **Out-the-door price** (OTD)
    
- Registration & title fees
    
- Dealer prep / freight charges
    
- Any hidden costs
    
- Financing terms (APR, monthly payment, total cost of credit)
    
- Required deposit
    

They’re asking themselves:

> “What will this actually cost me — all in?”

If the ad says $8,999, they’re wondering if it’s actually $11,200 by the time paperwork is done.

---

## 2. Condition & History (Especially for Used Bikes)

If it’s pre-owned, they want to know:

- Service history
    
- Accident history
    
- Number of previous owners
    
- Any cosmetic or mechanical issues
    
- Tire condition
    
- Brake life
    
- Upcoming maintenance due
    
- Warranty included?
    

For example, if it’s a used Harley-Davidson Street Glide, they want to know if it’s been well maintained or ridden hard.

Uncertainty about condition = hesitation.

---

## 3. Reliability & Ownership Costs

Even for new bikes like a Yamaha MT-07 or Honda CBR500R, buyers want to know:

- Is it reliable?
    
- What’s the maintenance schedule?
    
- How expensive are parts?
    
- Insurance cost range?
    
- Fuel consumption?
    
- Expected resale value?
    

They’re thinking long-term cost, not just purchase price.

---

## 4. Is It the Right Bike for Me?

This is more emotional and lifestyle-driven.

They want reassurance about:

- Seat height (can I flat-foot it?)
    
- Comfort for long rides
    
- Passenger comfort
    
- Highway capability
    
- Power delivery (beginner-friendly or aggressive?)
    
- Weight and maneuverability
    

Especially first-time buyers worry:

> “Am I buying the wrong bike?”

---

## 5. Availability & Delivery Time

If it’s new:

- Is it in stock?
    
- What color options are available?
    
- How long until delivery?
    
- Is there a waitlist?
    

Scarcity increases urgency — but uncertainty increases delay.

---

## 6. Trade-In Value

If they’re upgrading, they want:

- Rough trade-in estimate
    
- How it affects monthly payment
    
- Whether they’ll get lowballed
    

Trade-in transparency builds trust.

---

## 7. Financing Approval Odds

Many buyers won’t say this directly, but they’re wondering:

- Will I get approved?
    
- What credit score do I need?
    
- What will my monthly payment be?
    

If you can reduce anxiety around financing, you reduce friction.

---

## 8. Dealer Trust & After-Sales Support

Customers want to feel safe.

They care about:

- Dealer reputation
    
- Service department quality
    
- Warranty support
    
- Return policy
    
- First service included?
    
- Roadside assistance?
    

This is especially true for premium brands like BMW Motorrad or Ducati where service experience matters.

---

# The Psychology Behind It

Every question fits into one of three categories:

1. **Financial Risk** – “Can I afford this?”
    
2. **Mechanical Risk** – “Will this break or cost me later?”
    
3. **Emotional Risk** – “Am I making the right choice?”
    

If your advert answers 80% of these upfront, you reduce inbound friction dramatically.

---

# Why Customers Still Call Even After Seeing a Detailed Ad

Even when the ad is detailed, they still contact the dealership because they want:

- Personal reassurance
    
- Negotiation opportunity
    
- Clarification
    
- Human connection
    
- Validation of their decision
    

They are not just buying a motorcycle — they’re buying confidence.

# The 10 Most Common Customer Questions

## 1. “What’s the out-the-door price?”

Translation: _What will I actually pay?_

They want:

- Final total including fees
    
- Registration
    
- Dealer prep
    
- Freight
    
- Taxes
    
- Any hidden add-ons
    

This is almost always the first serious question.

---

## 2. “Is that price negotiable?”

Even if they don’t ask directly, they’re testing flexibility.

They’re really asking:

- Is there room?
    
- Are there current promotions?
    
- Can you match another offer?
    

---

## 3. “What would my monthly payment be?”

Even cash buyers ask this to benchmark value.

They want:

- Monthly payment
    
- Down payment required
    
- APR
    
- Term length
    
- Total cost of credit
    

Payment framing often matters more than total price.

---

## 4. “Has it ever been in an accident?” (Used bikes)

Followed by:

- How many owners?
    
- Full service history?
    
- Any major repairs?
    

Trust is everything here.

---

## 5. “How many miles/kilometers does it have?”

But what they’re really asking is:

- Is this high mileage for this model?
    
- Will I need major maintenance soon?
    

For example, a 25,000-mile Honda CBR500R may not worry experienced riders — but a first-time buyer might panic without reassurance.

---

## 6. “What warranty comes with it?”

They want to know:

- Manufacturer warranty remaining?
    
- Dealer warranty?
    
- Extended warranty options?
    
- What exactly is covered?
    

This question reduces mechanical risk anxiety.

---

## 7. “When was it last serviced?”

Follow-up concerns:

- Tire condition?
    
- Brake life?
    
- Chain/sprocket condition?
    
- Any maintenance due soon?
    

They’re calculating upcoming ownership cost.

---

## 8. “Is it good for beginners / long trips / two-up riding?”

This is about fit.

They’re wondering:

- Seat height?
    
- Weight?
    
- Power delivery?
    
- Comfort?
    
- Passenger setup?
    

Even experienced riders need validation.

---

## 9. “Can I test ride it?”

If they ask this, they’re emotionally invested.

What it really means:

- I’m close to buying.
    
- I need to feel it first.
    
- I need confidence.
    

---

## 10. “What will you give me for my trade-in?”

They want:

- Rough valuation
    
- How it affects payment
    
- Whether the dealer is fair
    

Trade-in transparency dramatically affects close rate.

---

# Bonus (Very Common but Not Always Spoken)

- “Why are you selling it?”
    
- “How long has it been sitting?”
    
- “Can you hold it for me?”
    
- “Are there any extra fees?”
    

---

# What This Means for Dealerships

Almost all these questions fall into three buckets:

1. **Total financial exposure**
    
2. **Mechanical risk**
    
3. **Fit & lifestyle suitability**
    

If your advert answers at least 7 of these before they ask, you:

- Reduce friction
    
- Shorten sales cycles
    
- Increase serious inquiries
    
- Filter out low-intent shoppers
