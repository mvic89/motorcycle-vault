##### Step by step process of customer motorcycle purchase:
- The customer comes in and wants to buy a bike, or he / she shows their interest in a bike through Blocket annons or through the AVA MC website itself (which Blocket is connected to).
- If the customer is interested in trying out the bike, they can fill in a 'test driving card'. It's an official permit issued by the dealer that allows a potential buyer to take the motorcycle out for a test ride before a purchase. AVA MC doesn't put the customer information in the system before the test ride materializes into a potential purchase.
- If the customer is interested in buying the bike, an 'offer' is created. The offer has all the customer information and additional information about the motorcycle / spare parts costs.
- If the customer accepts the offer, a 'purchase agreement' is created. 
- Once the purchase agreement has been signed, AVA MC starts to make the motorcycle ready for delivery (if it's in stock), or order the motorcycle / spare parts / equipment etc. if they don't have it in stock.
- There's 3 scenarios:
	- They can order the bike the customer wants directly from the motorcycle brand's ordering system. (if it's in stock of course)
	- They can link the customer to the pre-ordered bike if it's already pre-ordered.
	- If they have the motorcycle in stock, then it's sold. (which they usually try to convince the customer to go for.)
	 
- Insurance can be handled both before / after the agreement depending on the bike (if it's a sports motorcycle with a high insurance fee, then the customer might want to have the figures set beforehand).
- Registration is done after the purchase. (when down payment is paid and the vehicle is fully financed).
- When the motorcycle is ready to be delivered, the registration at 'Transportstyrelsen' through 'Bilvision' is made and warranties are registered through the motorcycle brand's portal. (different brands have different lengths on their warranties) (Kawasaki has 4-year warranties for instance.)
- Now the motorcycle is ready to be delivered or picked up by the customer.

#### How a motorcycle should be sold / traded in

- The customer comes in and wants to trade in a bike for either a new bike / newer version of the same bike / or money.
- Gunnar / Tony informs the customer of the price they're willing to pay for the bike, presupposed they make a full scale evaluation of the vehicle in the workshop.
- If the customer agrees, then AVA MC books the bike for an evaluation. (Customer comes in with the bike? or do AVA MC come by the customer to retrieve it?)
- The mechanics take a look at the overall condition of the vehicle (by taking photos of it), look up service information and fixes what needs to be fixed.
- The customer then receives a price offer with all the documented changes + the costs withdrawn from the price.
- If the customer accepts the deal, then a 'sales agreement' is signed and the money is paid out (Right on the spot? or through a bank transaction?) /bank transaction
- Registration, insurance and delivery of the traded in bike occurs when it's sold to a new customer.
- If the customer trades in the bike for another bike, then they pay the difference.
- If the customer wants AVA MC to sell the bike for them, then AVA MC takes out a deposit for the sale, advertise it but doesn't take ownership of it. (that's on the customer until the bike has been sold.)

##### How a motorcycle is sold / traded in today

Gunnar and Tony don't have time to make a full scale evaluation of the motorcycle, so they look up the value of the bike and set a price for it. If they're unsure of the bikes quality, they take it for a test drive to make sure there's nothing wrong with it. If it is, then they fix it up so that it's in good condition when it's sold to the next customer.

The problem is: if they let the mechanics work on the bike, the costs starts to ramp up due to all the findings that needs to be changed (washing, cleaning etc.). That takes a lot of time and the 'trade in' is usually done within a very small window of time. If Gunnar test drives the bike, that's time taken from assisting other customers needs.

A sales agreement that includes information on the amount of service required for the bike to be salable (säljbar) to the next customer needs to be in place before they evaluate the vehicle, because what if the mechanics starts to work on the bike and the customer haven't agreed to all the costs included? What if the person changes his mind and looks for other vendors who pays out more without any quality check.

It's important that there's no scratches or dents on the used motorcycle when it's sold to a new customer, but it's not an issue when it's traded in (they fix it up in the workshop).

##### Additional notes

When you trade in the motorcycle, the state of the vehicle is checked (tire wear, service documents etc.). With newer bikes, AVA takes a look at the receipts and double checks with the brand if service has been done. (every brand has their own service database). AVA har sin egna servicebok där dom kan se motocykelns service historik.

When the customer wants AVA MC to sell their bike for them, the customer is registered on the bike until it's been sold. AVA MC doesn't take ownership of the bike but helps out with finding potential buyers and advertising the bike on Blocket. (In extension their own website.)

Blocket and AVA MC's website are interconnected so when a motorcycle is in stock, they put up a 'Blocket Advert' with 'Blocket Admin' with all the bike information. After that, the advert data is sent and presented on the website.

If a customer is interested in one of AVA MC motorcycles on Blocket (or their own website), they correspond through email with Tony or Gunnar. This notice of interest is printed out and kept in a records file for a follow up.

As stated earlier, they put up the available motorcycles through 'Blocket Admin' on their website. this has to be done manually after the bikes has been registered into the database. It should be automatically done so that when they register a bike into the stock, it should automatically appear on the website (No manually created Blocket adverts needed!).

The customer usually pays the down payment through 'Swish' or 'Bankgiro' which is more beneficial for AVA MC due to lower banking fees. The possibility to use credit card still works even though it's discouraged.

Bankgiro is mostly used for later deliveries (not when the customer picks up the bike directly from the store). The account details are sent by email.

Tony wants to have the 'Bankgiro' information as well as 'Swish' and 'Account number' available on the purchase agreement.

Right now, the 'offer' and the 'purchase agreement' are combined into one document (which is the purchase agreement). Tony wants both the 'offer' document and the 'purchase agreement' to be two separate documents.

AVA MC doesn't trade in motorcycles older than 15 years, because older bikes usually have more problems that requires fixing. If they've been stored for a long time, then they need to take the vehicle apart and clean it, which takes time. (and time = money).

Gunnar and Tony sets a price for the traded-in motorcycle which they think the next buyer is willing to pay. They're known to always have the right price and to keep themselves on the lower price range on blocket. That way, there's no need for long-spun (utdragna) discussions about the price but instead a referal to blocket is enough.

Down payment can be split up monthly. The length depends on the motorcycle but used bikes have up 'til 5 years maximum while the newer ones can have a monthly payment up until 10 years. It's quite flexible so if a buyer happened to pay it off in 1 year instead of 5 years, they can end the payment agreement, and aren't required to pay interest for the next coming 4 years.
